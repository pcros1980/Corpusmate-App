About CorpusMate

CorpusMate is a language discovery tool powered by nine real-world corpora. It uses AI to help you explore how language varies across genres, registers, and contexts — from academic papers to fiction, news to game dialogue.

The nine corpora

BAWE — British Academic Written English
Undergraduate and postgraduate academic writing across disciplines.
Elsevier — Science Research Corpus
Published scientific journal articles — formal research writing across STEM fields.
BASE — British Academic Spoken English
Transcripts of university lectures and seminars — academic spoken register.
TED Talks
Transcripts of TED conference presentations — planned but conversational public speech.
BBC Teach
Educational content from the BBC Teach website — accessible journalistic register.
Movie Subtitles
Film and TV subtitle text — informal, spontaneous spoken language.
Novels — Literary Fiction Corpus
Literary fiction — narrative prose, description, and dialogue.
Games — Video Games Corpus
Video game scripts and interactive dialogue — a distinctive digital register.
Simple English Wikipedia
Plain-language encyclopaedic entries written for clarity and accessibility.

CorpusMate is powered by Cohere Command A and built for language learners, teachers, translators, and researchers. All queries are rate-limited to 100 per day per user to keep the service free.
